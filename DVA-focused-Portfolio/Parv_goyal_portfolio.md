## Problem Statement  

NASA tracks over 41,000 Near-Earth Asteroids, but raw datasets from JPL use abbreviated fields like `H`, `e`, `a`, `q`, `ad`, `per`, `n`, which are difficult to interpret for analysis.

The goal of this project was to transform and present this data in a way that highlights asteroid risk and supports decision-making.

**Core Question:**  
Which asteroids pose the highest risk to Earth, and how do orbital and velocity factors influence that risk?
